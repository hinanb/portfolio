In the real banking world, bill payments and interbank funds transfers follow well-defined processes. Here's how they work based on production experience.

## IBFT (Inter Bank Fund Transfer)

1. Customer initiates transfer with beneficiary account details
2. Bank validates sender account balance and limits
3. Request sent to central switch (e.g., 1LINK in Pakistan)
4. Beneficiary bank validates account and credits funds
5. Confirmation sent back through the switch
6. Both banks update transaction logs
7. Customer receives notification (SMS/push/email)

## Utility Bill Payment

1. Customer selects utility provider and enters consumer number
2. System fetches bill details from utility company API
3. Customer confirms amount and initiates payment
4. Funds debited from customer account
5. Payment forwarded to utility company
6. Receipt generated and notification sent

## Key Engineering Considerations

- **Idempotency** — Safe retry on network failures
- **Reconciliation** — Daily settlement with switch/utility providers
- **Timeout handling** — Reverse transactions on timeout
- **Audit trail** — Complete transaction logging for compliance
